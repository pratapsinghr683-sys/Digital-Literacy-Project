# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
**Task 4 – Part B | CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Rudra Pratap Singh | 25BAI10050**

---

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do | Why It Matters |
|---|-----|---------------|
| 1 | **Keep your profiles professional and clean** | Recruiters and internship coordinators actively search your name online before interviews |
| 2 | **Think before you post — ask "Would I be comfortable if my professor saw this?"** | What feels harmless today can surface years later during job applications or background checks |
| 3 | **Use privacy settings to control who sees your personal content** | Limiting personal posts to friends or followers reduces exposure to unknown third parties |
| 4 | **Give credit when sharing others' work or ideas** | Proper attribution is both ethical and demonstrates digital maturity |
| 5 | **Report and block accounts that send threatening or suspicious messages** | Reporting protects you and helps platforms remove bad actors faster |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Don't | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|---------------|
| 1 | **Don't post your phone number, home address, or college ID publicly** | This information can be misused for scams, fraud, or physical harm |
| 2 | **Don't share your OTP, UPI PIN, or banking details with anyone online — even if they claim to be from your bank** | No legitimate bank or service will ever ask for these details through social media or messages |
| 3 | **Don't engage in cyberbullying or pile-on behaviour in comment sections** | Online harassment has serious legal consequences in India under the IT Act, 2000 |
| 4 | **Don't share fake news or unverified information without checking the source** | Spreading misinformation can damage your reputation and, in some cases, carry legal liability |
| 5 | **Don't accept friend or follow requests from people you do not know personally** | Unknown connections increase your exposure to phishing, grooming, and social engineering attacks |
