## Cybercrime Type: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

### Background

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) has transformed how India conducts financial transactions, with over 13 billion transactions recorded monthly as of 2024. However, this widespread adoption has made it one of the most targeted areas for online fraud, particularly among young college students who are new to independent financial management.

---

### How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

**Step 1 – Target Selection:** The fraudster identifies a target, often through OLX, Facebook Marketplace, or hostel notice boards where students post items for sale (old laptops, textbooks, etc.).

**Step 2 – Initial Contact:** The fraudster contacts the student, pretending to be an interested buyer or occasionally a "bank representative" or "KYC update officer."

**Step 3 – Creating Urgency:** The fraudster claims they want to send payment immediately and asks the student to "accept" the payment via their UPI app.

**Step 4 – The Deception:** Instead of sending money, the fraudster shares a QR code or sends a "collect request" — which, if scanned or approved, DEDUCTS money from the victim's account rather than adding to it. Many students do not read the transaction screen carefully and approve it assuming it is an incoming payment.

**Step 5 – Disappearance:** Once the transaction is approved, the fraudster cuts contact immediately. The money cannot be recovered through the UPI app.

---

### Who Is Usually Targeted

First and second-year college students are disproportionately targeted because many are managing their own bank accounts for the first time, are unaware that a collect request deducts money, and are often selling items online in hostel communities.

---

### Consequences

Victims lose anywhere from ₹500 to ₹50,000 in a single transaction. Beyond the financial loss, victims often feel embarrassed and do not report the crime immediately, allowing the fraudster to target others. Delay in reporting reduces the chance of recovery significantly.
