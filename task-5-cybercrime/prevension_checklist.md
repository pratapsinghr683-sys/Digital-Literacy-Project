## General Online Safety

| # | Action | Detail |
|---|--------|--------|
| 1 | **Never click on links in unsolicited emails or SMS messages** | Phishing links look real but redirect to fake pages that steal your login credentials |
| 2 | **Use a strong, unique password for every account** | A strong password has at least 12 characters, with numbers, symbols, and upper/lowercase letters. Use a password manager if needed |
| 3 | **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on email, social media, and banking apps** | Even if your password is stolen, 2FA blocks unauthorised access without a second verification step |
| 4 | **Only download apps from the official Google Play Store or Apple App Store** | Third-party APK files often contain malware that can silently steal data from your device |
| 5 | **Lock your phone with a PIN, pattern, or biometric** | If your phone is lost or stolen, a lock screen prevents immediate access to UPI apps and email |
| 6 | **Regularly review app permissions on your smartphone** | An app that asks for microphone or location access when it does not need it may be collecting data unnecessarily |

---

## 💰 Financial & UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

| # | Action | Detail |
|---|--------|--------|
| 7 | **Always read the UPI screen carefully before approving any transaction — "Collect Request" TAKES money from you, it does NOT send money to you** | This is the most common UPI scam. A collect request on UPI means someone is requesting money FROM your account. Never approve one from an unknown contact |
| 8 | **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone — not even someone claiming to be from your bank, NPCI, or the government** | No legitimate institution will ever ask for these details. Anyone who asks is a fraudster |

---

## If You Are a Victim – How to Report

| Channel | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | https://cybercrime.gov.in — File a report online within 24 hours of the incident for the best chance of fund recovery |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** — Available 24x7. Call immediately after a fraud to initiate a freeze on the fraudster's account before the money is withdrawn |
| **Your Bank's Fraud Helpline** | Call the number on the back of your debit/credit card to report unauthorised transactions |

---

> **Remember:** Reporting quickly matters. The sooner you report, the higher the chance of recovery. There is no shame in being scammed — these fraudsters are professionals. The shame belongs only to the criminals.
